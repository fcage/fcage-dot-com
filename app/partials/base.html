Hi! I'm a journalist, designer and coder, working at The Guardian as a special projects editor. A typical week consists of producing and editing visual projects and overseeing the development of tools for the team. Work highlights include 2012 election results, the gay rights project, single-serve sites, and visualizing data with social and personalization hooks. More on that later.

Priors include AP, where I built an interactive template system (in Flash, yikes!) that allowed AP's global team to collaborate and more than double digital breaking news output. I also produced live 2010 midterm election maps than ran on more than 50 news sites. Before AP, I was a digital designer for TIME.

I studied journalism at the University of North Carolina and hold a masters in management from New York University.

Mekong: a river rising

This project was a mobile experiment with a card-based interface that looked to expose readers more broadly to the various sections of the project. Readers are given a general overview by swiping up, and options to explore a topic in more detail by swiping left.

  • View the project

I love experimenting with new story forms, but I think it's important to know what works and what doesn't. This includes ad hoc user testing before launch and user behavior tracking after launch.

Mekong: a river rising is a recent example where colleagues across several departments were asked to view multiple iterations of the project during development to understand the level of guidance readers needed to understand the card-based navigation. Post-launch analysis was used to understand that, yes indeed, this choose your own adventure experience was much more effective than a scrolly article on mobile.
